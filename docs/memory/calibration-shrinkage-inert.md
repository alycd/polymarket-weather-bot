FIXED 2026-06-03 (S1). The model's bucket probabilities are overconfident on the trades it selects: stated mean p_win ≈ 0.866 vs realized win rate ≈ 0.747 (12-pt gap) over 76 resolved tradeable-city trades. The shrinkage correction was dormant for TWO reasons, both fixed in `metrics/calibration.py`:
1. **Taxonomy mismatch:** temperature trades are recorded with `market_type="daily"`, but `update_shrinkage_factors()` only iterated `("temperature","tsa","crypto")` and `edge_calculator` reads `get_shrinkage_factor("temperature")` → factor was never computed nor found. Fix: `SHRINKAGE_FAMILIES` maps daily/weekly→temperature.
2. **Never scheduled:** the daemon never ran `--calibration`. Fix: added a daily `(8,5,"--calibration")` event to `daemon.py`.

The raw calibration slope is ~0.5 (very overconfident), but combined with the `MIN_WIN_PROB=0.70` entry gate that already filters overconfident bets it double-penalises and starves volume — so the shrink is floored at `SHRINKAGE_FLOOR=0.75` (empirically maximises realised PnL + win-rate in trade replay; relaxes toward 1.0 as calibration improves). Now stored: `kv_store.cal_shrinkage_temperature=0.75`. Replay at 0.75: win 74%→76%, ROI +27%→+36%, Brier −7%.

This is edge-driven adverse selection, not general miscalibration: over ALL enumerated buckets the model is well-calibrated (Brier ~0.072), but the bot selects buckets where it most disagrees with the market, and on exactly those the market (pricing in bias/microclimate) is more right. See [[excluded-cities-are-bias-driven]] and [[bias-correction-dominates-ensemble-weights]].

**UPDATE 2026-06-09 — shrinkage did NOT fix it; overconfidence is WORSE and the floor is stuck.** Over 154 settled trades: stated 86% vs actual **63%** (gap +23, ~double the original +12). The "97% confident" bucket wins **57.8%**; YES bets 96% stated vs **43%** actual. The `--calibration` job runs daily (factor refreshed on schedule) but `cal_shrinkage_temperature` is **pinned at the 0.75 floor** — the true selected-bet slope is ~0.17, so it wants to shrink far harder but is clamped. **Lowering SHRINKAGE_FLOOR was tested via replay and REJECTED**: dropping it cuts PnL monotonically (+$83→−$97 at 0.75→0.50) with NO win-rate gain — because the miscalibration is so severe that gating on *stated* confidence is nearly uninformative about outcomes. Instead, targeted guards shipped (paper, 2026-06-09): cap `MAX_EDGE_ABS=0.40` (|edge|>0.40 wins only 38%, −17.5% ROI — adverse-selection noise), neutralized the 2× `HIGH_CONVICTION_KELLY_MULT` boost (was amplifying that tail), and paused YES (`ENABLE_YES_BETS=False`). The deeper unfixed issue: the model's confident disagreements with the market are mostly noise; the real lever is upstream (probability distribution too sharp on selected bets), not post-hoc shrinkage. See [[payoff-asymmetry-levers-exhausted]].

**UPDATE 2026-06-09 (S2) — the "upstream widening" lever is REJECTED; overconfidence is a SELECTION artifact, not a distribution-sharpness one.** Built the offline Brier harness the prior session asked for: joined `model_forecasts` (lead 0 & 1) to `historical_obs` actuals → **783 forecast-days** with real NOAA outcomes (vs only 19 actuals in the trades table), replicated the real ensemble (weighted mean, Kish spread, `dynamic_base_std`, Student-t df=4) and swept an inflation factor k on `effective_std`. Result: **full-distribution Brier is MINIMIZED at k=1.00** and rises monotonically with inflation (lead-0 0.0468→0.0515 at k=2.0). **Modal-bucket calibration shows UNDER-confidence:** stated P(temp in modal bucket) 19.3%/16.9% (lead 0/1) vs actual hit 33.4%/27.8% — the temp lands in the model's central bucket MORE often than stated. So the model is well-calibrated (mildly under-confident) on the *population* of forecasts; the "86%/62%" gap is purely **bet selection** (the bot bets NO on low-prob buckets where it most disagrees with the market, and the market is right there). Do NOT widen `BASE_FORECAST_STD_C`/`effective_std` — it worsens Brier and would hurt the profitable T+0 cohort. The real exploitable structure is lead time, not distribution width: see [[tplus1-leadtime-regime-split]].

## Diagnosis (the data)

- **Profitable core:** tradeable cities = 75% win, +27.1% ROI, +$311. Excluded cities = 32% win, −49.8% ROI, −$239. The exclusion list was doing real work.
- **Overconfidence via adverse selection:** over *all* enumerated buckets the model is well-calibrated (Brier ~0.072). But the bot selects the buckets where it most *disagrees* with the market — and there the market (which prices in bias/microclimate the naive Student-t misses) is more right. Hence the biggest "edges" (0.40+) were the *worst* trades (38% win).
- **Calibration curve (resolved trades):** predicted 95% → actual 55%; predicted 85% → actual 71%. Clear, confidence-increasing overconfidence.
- **Forecast error is mostly BIAS, not spread:** raw ensemble std (1.43°C) ≈ the bot's assumed effective_std, so spread is fine. But there's a **+0.53°C universal cold bias** plus per-city biases up to ±2–3°C (Atlanta +1.9, Seattle +2.9, Seoul +1.9, SF +2.0). On a 1°C bucket, a biased mean makes the model confidently wrong about *which* bucket.
- **Ensemble weighting is nearly irrelevant** (−0.6% OOS); bias correction dominates ~30:1. (ICON is the single best model — RMSE 1.62 — beating the 1.8×-weighted ECMWF; noted but not acted on.)

## S1 — Calibration shrinkage (now ACTIVE)

**Files:** `metrics/calibration.py`, `daemon.py`

The shrink-toward-0.5 correction (`get_shrinkage_factor` → applied in `signals/edge_calculator.py`) was dead for **two** reasons, both fixed:

1. **Taxonomy mismatch.** Temperature trades are recorded with `market_type="daily"`, but `update_shrinkage_factors()` only iterated `("temperature","tsa","crypto")` and `edge_calculator` reads `get_shrinkage_factor("temperature")` → the factor was never computed *and* never found.
   - Fix: added `SHRINKAGE_FAMILIES = {"temperature": ("temperature","daily","weekly"), "tsa": (...), "crypto": (...)}` and grouped by family.
2. **Never scheduled.** The daemon never ran `--calibration`.
   - Fix: added `(8, 5, "--calibration", "Daily calibration + shrinkage refresh")` to `daemon.MODEL_RUN_EVENTS`.

**Floor = 0.75** (`SHRINKAGE_FLOOR`). The raw calibration slope is ~0.5 (very overconfident), but the existing `MIN_WIN_PROB=0.70` entry gate already filters overconfident bets, so a 0.5 shrink double-penalizes and starves volume. Replay sweep showed total PnL peaks ~0.75:

```
shrink   N    win%   ROI     PnL     $/trade
1.00     76   74%   +27.2%   $291    +3.83   (baseline)
0.80     52   73%   +30.7%   $225    +4.33
0.75     46   76%   +36.3%   $238    +5.17   <-- chosen
0.70     30   80%   +43.6%   $188    +6.26
0.65     24   83%   +44.7%   $152    +6.35
```

It self-tunes: stored as `kv_store.cal_shrinkage_temperature=0.75`, and the daily job will relax it toward 1.0 as calibration improves.

**Tradeoff:** it's a precision/volume knob — at 0.75 it forgoes some profitable lower-edge volume to concentrate on better trades and right-size Kelly stakes. S2 adds volume back via new cities.

## How `--backfill` ties in (and what refreshes what)

`--backfill` (`cmd_backfill`) does NOT recompute the calibration/shrinkage factor — it only
refreshes **bias corrections** (S2). Its step 5 is `recompute_bias(icao)` for **every city in
`CITIES`** (full dict, not the tradeable subset), rebuilt on up to 180 days of obs + 92 days of
backfilled forecasts. So running `--backfill` strengthens S2 and pre-populates corrections for
not-yet-traded re-admitted cities. The shrinkage factor (S1) is untouched by backfill.

| Command | Refreshes bias (S2)? | Refreshes shrinkage (S1)? |
|---|---|---|
| `--backfill` | ✅ all cities | ❌ |
| `--calibration` | ❌ | ✅ |
| `--resolve` | ✅ only cities with a resolving trade | ❌ |

**Manual full refresh:** `python main.py --backfill` then `python main.py --calibration`.

## Daemon schedule (when maintenance runs)

Both jobs are placed in scan-free **:15** gaps (the 30-min opportunistic + exit scans fire on
:00/:30; fixed scans at 07:13/09:45/13:30/19:17, resolves 07:47/19:55, nowcasts cluster
~05–07:30 / 12–14:30 / 18–22:30 UTC). The daemon is single-threaded/sequential, so nothing
ever runs concurrently anyway.

- **`--calibration` — daily 08:15 UTC** (after the 07:47 morning resolve settles trades). Keeps
  `cal_shrinkage_temperature` current; relaxes toward 1.0 as calibration improves.
- **`--backfill` — weekly, Sunday 03:15 UTC** (`WEEKLY_EVENTS` in `daemon.py`; quiet window,
  worst case delays one 03:30 opportunistic/exit scan). Keeps every city's bias corrections
  fresh, even cities that haven't traded.
